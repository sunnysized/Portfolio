MY FAMILY AND FRIENDS CALL ME SUNNY

Hi there!
My name is Sonal Patel, but everyone pretty much calls me Sunny. In May of 2018, I graduated from Texas Christian University with my bachelor of science in Communication Studies and a minor in General Business. I then went to work for a brief amount of time making the exhaustive commute from Plano to Downtown Dallas to work for an artificial intelligence software company that happened to work closely in the automotive realm. After about five to six months, I heard about the bootcamp offered through SMU to become a full stack developer. I was very quickly interested and found myself applying right after the new year, entering 2019. Come February 25th, I was officially a aprt of the Feburary cohort! Six months later, I am writing my new polished portfolio, interviewing with HP and Cisco, and working on the logistics of turning my project 3 assignment into a nonprofit organization which will change the nation. Hell, I may even get an MBA while I'm at it!

Enough about my academic and semi-professional life! The point of this page is to get to know me on a personal level. So, here we go! Growing up I was a super happy-go-lucky child whose teachers would describe me on every report card as a "social butterfly". If you're reading this and you know me personally- not much of a shocker, haha. I played volleyball through middle school and had the best underhand serve! Overhand, though, I was best friends with the net. (If you don't know volleyball, you don't want your serve to go into the net- ever.) Aside from volleyball, I have been a dancer since I was three years old. This year, I have been dancing for 20 years recreationally. I competed during my high school years and won a few scholarships in LA. I am trained in ballet, tap, jazz, hip hop, street, contemporary, and both contact and traditional improv. Dancing has been an outlet for me since I could walk. As I went through my college years, I dabbled a little bit in the modeling industry. I have recently done a photoshoot for a boutqiue specializing in Indian attire which you can see a bit at the bottom of this page!

So we've covered academic, semi professional, childhood, so now I dive in a bit deeper: my family. These are my people. My entire world. If it weren't for my dad, I probably wouldn't even been writing this right now. He sparked my desire for knowledge, cars, thrill seeking adventure, and is at the core of my silly yet strong headed personality. If you ask anyone who knows my father, after a single encounter with me they always say, "you two are SO alike!" And they are not wrong. I love my dad so much, but at the end of the day, I am mommy's girl! (Don't worry, I have a sister who's daddy's girl- we'll get to her in a minute). My mom is my IDOL. My dad may be at my core, but that woman is everything I strive to be. My dad is my foundation and my mother is who I radiate. She is the strongest woman I know, who is just like me! We are two peas in a pod. She is my go to person. My teacher, my guide, and not to mention the best chef in the world. She raised my sister and I, worked full time, and every night put a hot meal on the dinner table for us. She taught us how to clean the house as a team on the weekends as children, how to cook Indian meals that we especially liked, let us put on plays and skits we'd come up with. She was present and still bust her ass. She's my wonder woman... So that just leaves my baby sister. THIS GIRL IS MY UNIVERSE. Kailas Patel. I picked your name out before you were born. Made sure your initials were KP after Kim Possible, my favorite show when I was four years old. And I protected you as if I was Kim Possible too. We laugh more than we probably speak. We make silly sounds, we sing Ariana Grande together at the top of our lungs, even when I know you'd rather I not sing, haha. We played volleyball together in the yard, we've done photoshoots together, raised hell, and there is not a soul in the world I'd want by my side to do it all with. Even when life gets the best of either of us, we are each others support system. When we are scared, or stressed, or just need to freaking cry- we always have each others shoulder. We are each others keepers, and I am so proud of have you as my teammate in this life! My family is the most important group of people and I am pretty damn grateful for them.

I also have a boyfriend and a kitten. They're both ridiculously amazing, but my computer is dying so they will get their own entry at a later time! (Haha, sorry!)
