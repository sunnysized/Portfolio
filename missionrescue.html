our mission: rescue victims

The Facts
A person is abused in the United States every NINE seconds.
More than 15 MILLION children witness domestic violence each year in the United States.
Only 28% of victims report their sexual assault to the police.
We can change these statistics.

The Mission
We will provide an escape for victims across all forms of abuse. From domestic abuse, physical abuse, sexual abuse, child abuse and neglect we will show these victims support and give them a safe place to come for help. The mission is to educate and provide help. To extend a hand to someone who needs our support where they may not have it elsewhere. Our website may be the first place a survivor feels couragous enough to seek outside help. Our mission is to be a voice to those who feel muted. Our goal is to rescue victims and support them. Because after all they have been through, while they see themselves as victims, as hurt and scared... we will see them as survivors.

Functionality
Users will access the site via a button which can be imbedded onto any website. Upon access to the site, the user will be prompted with a login/sign up page. Once granted permission to the site various tabs of imformation are available for the user to explore. If the user feels uncomfortable or in danger of someone watching over their shoulder there is a double click escape where the user can double click anywhere on the window and the window will switch from our site to google and pinterest. On the home page there are six tabs: Our Cause, About Us, In Home, Out of Home, Worried about Others, and Advocating for Minors. Each tab will go in further detail of their respected topic. In the future we'd like to have LMSW's work for us as volunteers to provide immediate help via chat options that will be implemented in future versions of the website. In addition, we'd like to add a section on Title IX as well as offer talks in high schools to inform these individuals going through prominent developmental stages of their lives so that those statistics above, especially the 28% of sexual abuse victims, will increase when it comes to reporting their encounters to the police.
